# Skeletal Trauma — Questions

> DNB short-note format, 10 marks each. Mark splits suggested in brackets.

## Practice questions

**Q1.** Classify thoracolumbar spine fractures. Discuss the imaging findings and criteria for instability of a burst fracture. `[4+(3+3)]`

**Q2.** A young adult is brought after a road-traffic accident with an unstable pelvic injury. Describe the **Young-Burgess classification** and the role of imaging including the role of interventional radiology in pelvic haemorrhage. `[5+3+2]`

**Q3.** Describe the **Salter-Harris classification** of physeal injuries with diagrams. What is the prognostic significance of each type? `[5+5]`

**Q4.** Enumerate the imaging modalities for suspected **scaphoid fracture**. Why is MRI superior, and what are the complications of a missed fracture? `[3+3+4]`

**Q5.** Short notes: (a) Lipohaemarthrosis `[5]`  (b) Insufficiency fractures of the sacrum `[5]`

## Model-answer pointers

- **Q1:** Denis 3-column → define burst (anterior+middle, retropulsion). Instability: >2 columns, canal compromise, kyphosis, PLC injury (TLICS). Modality: XR → CT (bony, retropulsion %) → MRI (cord, PLC, marrow oedema).
- **Q2:** APC/LC/VS/combined with mechanisms; XR (inlet/outlet), CT angio for blush; embolisation for arterial bleed, external fixation/pelvic binder for venous.
- **Q3:** Diagram I-V; II commonest; V worst (growth arrest); III/IV intra-articular → anatomical reduction.
- **Q4:** XR (scaphoid views) → repeat at 10-14 d / CT / **MRI** (immediate marrow oedema, highest sensitivity & specificity). Missed → non-union, AVN of proximal pole, SNAC wrist.
- **Q5:** (a) fat-fluid level on horizontal-beam film = intra-articular fracture marrow fat. (b) elderly/osteoporotic, H-shaped uptake (Honda sign), MRI marrow oedema, no history of trauma.

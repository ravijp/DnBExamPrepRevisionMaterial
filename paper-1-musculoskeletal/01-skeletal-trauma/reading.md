# Skeletal Trauma — Spine, Pelvis, Appendicular

> Always frame the answer: **mechanism -> stability -> classification -> role of each modality.**
> XR = screening; **CT = gold standard for bony detail / complex fractures**; **MRI = ligaments, cord, marrow oedema (occult fracture).**

## Spine

**Cervical**
- Upper cervical eponyms: **Jefferson** (C1 burst, lateral mass spread on open-mouth view), **Hangman's** (C2 bilateral pars / traumatic spondylolisthesis), **odontoid fracture — Anderson & D'Alonzo I-III** (type II at base = commonest, high non-union risk).
- Subaxial: **SLIC** score (morphology + disco-ligamentous complex + neurology).

**Thoracolumbar**
- **Denis three-column concept**: anterior (anterior 2/3 body + ALL), middle (posterior 1/3 body + PLL), posterior (posterior elements). **Disruption of >=2 columns = unstable.**
- **AO / TLICS** classification (morphology + PLC integrity + neurology guides management).
- Types: **compression** (anterior column), **burst** (anterior + middle, retropulsion into canal), **Chance** (flexion-distraction, horizontal split, seat-belt injury), **fracture-dislocation** (all 3 columns).
- CT signs of instability: >50% body height loss, canal compromise, interspinous/facet widening, perched/locked facets.

## Pelvis

- **Young-Burgess (mechanistic):** **APC I-III** (open-book; symphyseal diastasis), **LC I-III** (lateral compression), **vertical shear (VS)**, **combined**. APC-III and VS carry the highest haemorrhage risk -> CT angiography for arterial blush -> **angio-embolisation.**
- **Tile A/B/C:** A stable, B rotationally unstable, C rotationally + vertically unstable.
- **Acetabulum — Judet & Letournel:** 2 columns (anterior/posterior), 2 walls; CT + 3D reconstruction essential for surgical planning.

## Appendicular & paediatric

- **Salter-Harris I-V** (physeal injuries) — mnemonic **SALTR**: I Slipped, II Above (commonest), III Lower, IV Through (both), V Rammed (crush, worst prognosis).
- Common eponyms: Colles/Smith, Monteggia vs Galeazzi, Bennett, Lisfranc, Maisonneuve.
- **Stress fractures**: fatigue (normal bone, abnormal stress) vs **insufficiency** (abnormal bone, normal stress; e.g. sacral **H/Honda sign** on bone scan/MRI).
- **Occult fractures** (scaphoid, hip): MRI most sensitive (marrow oedema).

## Pearls / buzzwords
- "Fat-fluid level (lipohaemarthrosis)" on horizontal-beam knee XR = intra-articular fracture.
- Segond fracture -> ACL tear association. Posterior fat-pad sign (elbow) -> occult fracture.
- Always comment on **soft tissues, alignment, joint, and an associated second injury** (spine fractures are often multiple).

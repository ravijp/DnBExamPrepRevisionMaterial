**Q4.** Short notes: (a) Osteoid osteoma `[5]`  (b) Telangiectatic osteosarcoma `[5]`

**Q5.** Discuss the imaging features of **Ewing sarcoma** and its differential diagnosis. `[6+4]`

## Model-answer pointers

- **Q1:** Bone-forming = osteoid osteoma, osteoblastoma, osteosarcoma (+subtypes). Osteosarcoma: metaphysis, osteoid matrix, aggressive periosteum, soft-tissue mass. MRI: marrow/skip/joint/NV bundle; CT chest mets.
- **Q2:** Pain, size >5 cm, deep endosteal scalloping >2/3, cortical thickening/breach, soft-tissue mass, marrow oedema → chondrosarcoma. Location (hand = usually enchondroma) helps.
- **Q3:** Cortico-medullary continuity; transformation if post-maturity growth, pain, cap >1.5-2 cm, lucent areas, soft-tissue mass.
- **Q4:** (a) nidus <1.5 cm + reactive sclerosis, night pain relieved by NSAIDs, CT/RFA. (b) lytic expansile + fluid-fluid levels, mimics ABC but aggressive features/soft-tissue mass.
- **Q5:** Diaphysis/flat bones, permeative, onion-skin periosteum, large soft-tissue mass; DDx osteomyelitis, lymphoma, metastatic neuroblastoma, Langerhans cell histiocytosis.

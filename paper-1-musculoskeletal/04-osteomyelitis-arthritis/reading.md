# Osteomyelitis & Septic / Granulomatous Arthritis

## Acute pyogenic osteomyelitis
- **Routes:** haematogenous (children — metaphysis, rich slow-flow sinusoids), contiguous spread, direct inoculation. Organism: *S. aureus* (Salmonella in sickle cell).
- **XR lags 7-14 days:** soft-tissue swelling → periosteal reaction → ill-defined lucency/destruction.
- **MRI is most sensitive early:** marrow oedema (**low T1, high T2/STIR, enhancement**), subperiosteal/soft-tissue abscess, sinus tract.
- Bone scan (3-phase) and labelled-WBC/PET useful where MRI is limited (metalwork, diabetic foot).

## Chronic osteomyelitis — know the terms (draw them)
- **Sequestrum** — devitalised dead bone (sclerotic, surrounded by lucency).
- **Involucrum** — living periosteal new bone surrounding sequestrum.
- **Cloaca** — opening in involucrum draining pus; **sinus tract** to skin.
- **Brodie abscess** — subacute, lucent lesion + sclerotic rim, may show **penumbra sign** (T1 hyperintense granulation rim).
- **Sclerosing osteomyelitis of Garré.**

## Diabetic foot / pressure sores
- Differentiate osteomyelitis from neuropathic (Charcot) joint: MRI signs — secondary signs of OM (skin ulcer, sinus tract, soft-tissue abscess, "ghost sign"); Charcot favours midfoot, deformity, subchondral cysts.

## Granulomatous (tuberculous)
- **Spine = Pott's disease** (commonest skeletal TB): begins anterior vertebral body, **relative disc preservation early**, **subligamentous spread to multiple levels, large paravertebral / psoas "cold" abscess (often calcified), gibbus deformity, vertebra plana**. **MRI is the investigation of choice** (extent, cord compression, abscess).
- **TB arthritis — Phemister triad:** juxta-articular osteoporosis, peripherally located erosions, gradual joint-space narrowing. Large weight-bearing joints; "kissing sequestra".

## Septic arthritis vs transient synovitis (paediatric hip)
- Septic: painful effusion, restricted movement, raised CRP/WBC → **US (effusion) + US-guided aspiration is diagnostic/therapeutic**; MRI shows synovial enhancement, marrow oedema if coexistent OM. Transient synovitis is a diagnosis of exclusion.

## Pearls / buzzwords
- "Penumbra sign" → Brodie abscess. "Cold abscess + relative disc sparing + subligamentous spread" → spinal TB.
- Early OM: normal XR does **not** exclude — get MRI.

## What to draw
Chronic OM diagram labelling sequestrum / involucrum / cloaca / sinus.

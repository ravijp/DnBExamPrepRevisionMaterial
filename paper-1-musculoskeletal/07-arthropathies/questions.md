# Arthropathies — Questions

## Practice questions

**Q1.** Describe the radiological features that differentiate **osteoarthritis from rheumatoid arthritis** of the hand. `[5+5]`

**Q2.** Discuss the imaging features of **seronegative spondyloarthropathies**, including sacroiliac joint involvement. `[10]`

**Q3.** Describe the radiological features of **gout**. How does it differ from rheumatoid arthritis? `[6+4]`

**Q4.** What is **chondrocalcinosis**? Enumerate its causes and describe the arthropathy of CPPD. `[2+3+5]`

**Q5.** Short notes: (a) Atlanto-axial subluxation in RA `[5]`  (b) Role of MRI/US in early inflammatory arthritis `[5]`

## Model-answer pointers

- **Q1:** OA productive/asymmetric/osteophytes/no osteopenia (DIP/PIP/CMC); RA symmetric/periarticular osteopenia/marginal erosions/uniform narrowing (MCP/PIP/wrist).
- **Q2:** AS (symmetric sacroiliitis, bamboo spine, syndesmophytes, enthesitis), psoriatic (asymmetric, pencil-in-cup, periostitis), reactive; SI grading.
- **Q3:** Punched-out erosions with overhanging margins, tophi, preserved space/density, 1st MTP; DECT urate; vs RA (osteopenia, marginal erosions, symmetric).
- **Q4:** Calcium in cartilage; causes (idiopathic/age, hyperparathyroidism, haemochromatosis, hypophosphatasia, gout); CPPD arthropathy in radiocarpal/patellofemoral, SLAC.
- **Q5:** (a) >3 mm atlanto-dental interval, flexion view/MRI, cord risk. (b) US power Doppler synovitis, MRI marrow oedema/erosions earlier than XR.

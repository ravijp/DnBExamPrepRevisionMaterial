# Arthropathies (Inflammatory, Crystal, Degenerative)

> The marks are in **distribution + erosion type**. Lead with a pattern, then give the differentiating features. Draw a hand showing the distribution.

## Degenerative — osteoarthritis (OA)
- **Productive** disease: **asymmetric, non-uniform joint-space narrowing, osteophytes, subchondral sclerosis, subchondral cysts (geodes)**; no erosions, no osteopenia.
- Distribution: DIP (Heberden), PIP (Bouchard), 1st CMC, hips, knees, spine. Erosive OA = central erosions, "gull-wing".

## Inflammatory
- **Rheumatoid arthritis (RA):** **symmetric**, MCP/PIP/wrist; **periarticular osteopenia, uniform joint-space narrowing, marginal erosions** (bare areas), soft-tissue swelling, ulnar deviation/subluxation, **atlanto-axial subluxation** (>3 mm). MRI/US: pannus, synovitis (power Doppler), early erosions.
- **Seronegative spondyloarthropathies:**
  - **Ankylosing spondylitis:** bilateral **symmetric sacroiliitis** → **bamboo spine** (thin marginal syndesmophytes), shiny corners (Romanus), squaring, Andersson lesions, enthesitis.
  - **Psoriatic:** DIP / ray pattern, asymmetric, **"pencil-in-cup", fluffy periostitis, no osteopenia**, "sausage digit", arthritis mutilans; asymmetric sacroiliitis.
  - **Reactive (Reiter):** lower limb, calcaneal enthesopathy.

## Crystal arthropathies
- **Gout:** asymmetric, **1st MTP (podagra)**; **punched-out erosions with sclerotic/overhanging edges**, **preserved joint space & bone density until late**, soft-tissue **tophi** (± dense on **dual-energy CT** for urate).
- **CPPD (pseudogout):** **chondrocalcinosis** (knee menisci, TFCC of wrist, symphysis pubis), structural arthropathy in unusual joints (radiocarpal → **SLAC-like** wrist, patellofemoral).
- **HADD (hydroxyapatite):** calcific tendinitis (supraspinatus).

## Comparison table

| | OA | RA | Psoriatic | Gout |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Symmetry | Asymmetric | **Symmetric** | Asymmetric | Asymmetric |
| Bone density | Normal | **Periarticular osteopenia** | Normal | Normal |
| Erosions | None (osteophytes) | Marginal | "Pencil-in-cup", periostitis | Punched-out, overhanging |
| Distribution | DIP/CMC/weight-bearing | MCP/PIP/wrist | DIP/ray | 1st MTP |

## Pearls / buzzwords
- "Overhanging edge erosion + preserved density" → gout.
- "Pencil-in-cup + fluffy periostitis" → psoriatic. "Chondrocalcinosis" → CPPD.
- Comment on **soft tissues, distribution, density, erosions** in every arthritis answer.

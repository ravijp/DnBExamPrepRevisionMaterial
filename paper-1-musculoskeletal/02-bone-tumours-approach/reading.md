# Bone Tumours — The Systematic Approach

> This "approach to a bone lesion" question is one of the **most repeated MSK themes**. The marks are in the *system*, not the diagnosis.

## The seven-step approach (write these as the framework)

1. **Age** — single best discriminator (e.g. <30 yr benign-leaning + Ewing/osteosarcoma; >40 yr metastasis/myeloma/chondrosarcoma).
2. **Bone & site within bone** — long vs flat; **epiphysis / metaphysis / diaphysis**; central / eccentric / cortical / juxtacortical.
3. **Zone of transition** — narrow & sclerotic = non-aggressive; wide & imperceptible = aggressive.
4. **Margin — Lodwick grading**: IA geographic sclerotic rim → IB geographic well-defined → IC ill-defined → **II moth-eaten** → **III permeative** (most aggressive).
5. **Periosteal reaction** — benign (solid/buttress) vs aggressive (lamellated/onion-skin, spiculated/sunburst/hair-on-end, **Codman triangle**).
6. **Matrix** — **osteoid** (cloud-like/ivory) vs **chondroid** (rings-and-arcs, popcorn, comma).
7. **Soft-tissue component** (best on MRI) — favours malignancy.

## Benign vs malignant (the comparison table)

| Feature | Non-aggressive | Aggressive |
|---|---|---|
| Margin | Geographic, sclerotic | Moth-eaten / permeative |
| Transition zone | Narrow | Wide |
| Periosteal reaction | Solid / none | Interrupted, spiculated, Codman |
| Cortex | Intact / expanded | Destroyed |
| Soft-tissue mass | Absent | Present |

## Location shortcuts (enumerate)
- **Epiphysis:** chondroblastoma (immature), **GCT** (mature, subarticular, eccentric), clear-cell chondrosarcoma, infection, geode.
- **Metaphysis:** osteosarcoma, NOF, SBC, ABC, chondromyxoid fibroma.
- **Diaphysis:** Ewing, lymphoma, fibrous dysplasia, adamantinoma, osteoid osteoma.

## Metastases (commonest malignant bone lesion in adults)
- Usually **>40 yr, multiple, axial (red marrow)**.
- Lytic: lung, kidney, thyroid, GI, breast. Blastic: prostate, treated breast, carcinoid. Mixed.
- Work-up: **bone scintigraphy** (sensitive screen, "superscan", flare phenomenon), **whole-body MRI / PET-CT** (marrow, lytic lesions, treatment response).

## Pearls / buzzwords
- "Fallen fragment sign" → simple bone cyst with pathological fracture.
- "Ground-glass matrix" → fibrous dysplasia.
- Multiple myeloma: punched-out lytic lesions, **photopenic on bone scan** (use skeletal survey / MRI / PET).

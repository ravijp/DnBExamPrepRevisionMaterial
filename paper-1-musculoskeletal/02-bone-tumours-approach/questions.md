## Model-answer pointers

- **Q1:** Seven-step framework; benign vs malignant table.
- **Q2:** Distribution (axial/red marrow); XR (only when >50% trabecular loss) → bone scan (screen, superscan, flare) → CT (cortical, complications) → MRI/PET (marrow, response). Lytic vs blastic primaries.
- **Q3:** Solid/buttress, lamellated (onion-skin → Ewing), spiculated/sunburst (osteosarcoma), Codman triangle (interrupted, aggressive); hair-on-end.
- **Q4:** Chondroblastoma, GCT, infection, clear-cell chondrosarcoma, geode. GCT: mature skeleton, subarticular, eccentric, lytic, narrow zone, no matrix, may be locally aggressive/recur; "soap-bubble".
- **Q5:** (a) IA-III progression. (b) punched-out lesions, diffuse osteopenia, vertebral collapse, photopenic on bone scan → skeletal survey/MRI/PET; raised paraprotein.

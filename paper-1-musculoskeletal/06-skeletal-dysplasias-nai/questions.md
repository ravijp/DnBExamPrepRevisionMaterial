**Q5.** Describe imaging features of **mucopolysaccharidoses (dysostosis multiplex)**. `[10]`

## Model-answer pointers

- **Q1:** Rhizomelic, narrowing interpedicular distance, short pedicles/stenosis, champagne-glass pelvis, frontal bossing, trident hand.
- **Q2:** CML (corner/bucket-handle), posterior ribs, scapula/sternum/spinous; different-age fractures; skeletal survey (dedicated views) + repeat at 2 wks; dating; DDx OI/rickets.
- **Q3:** Osteopetrosis, pyknodysostosis, sclerosing dysplasias, fluorosis, renal osteodystrophy, metastatic (rare in child). Describe e.g. osteopetrosis (bone-in-bone) + Engelmann (diaphyseal).
- **Q4:** (a) thin bones, fractures of varying age, wormian bones, blue sclera, vertebral collapse. (b) PORKCHOPS mnemonic — OI, cleidocranial dysplasia, pyknodysostosis, hypothyroidism, etc.
- **Q5:** J-sella, oar ribs, beaked vertebrae, V-metacarpals, thick calvarium, widened diaphyses.

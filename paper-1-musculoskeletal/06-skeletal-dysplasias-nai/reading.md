# Skeletal Dysplasias & Non-Accidental Injury

## Approach to a skeletal dysplasia
Describe: **which part of the bone is affected** (epiphyseal / physeal / metaphyseal / diaphyseal / spondylo-) and **the segment of limb shortening** (rhizomelic = proximal, mesomelic = middle, acromelic = distal, micromelic = whole).

## Must-know dysplasias (enumerate + one-line each)
- **Achondroplasia** (commonest non-lethal): rhizomelic shortening, **interpedicular distance narrows caudally (L1→L5)**, short pedicles → spinal stenosis, **"champagne-glass" pelvis**, frontal bossing, trident hand.
- **Osteogenesis imperfecta:** multiple fractures, **gracile/thin bones**, **wormian bones**, blue sclera, bowing, vertebral compression.
- **Osteopetrosis** ("marble bone"): diffusely dense, brittle bones, **"bone-in-bone" appearance, "sandwich/rugger-jersey" vertebra, Erlenmeyer flask** metaphyseal modelling.
- **Mucopolysaccharidoses** (Hurler etc.): **J-shaped sella, oar-shaped ribs, beaked/hook vertebrae, V-shaped metacarpals**.
- **Cleidocranial dysplasia:** absent/hypoplastic clavicles, delayed cranial ossification (wormian bones, open fontanelles), dental anomalies, wide pubic symphysis.
- **Diaphyseal sclerosing** dysplasias (e.g. Camurati-Engelmann — symmetric diaphyseal cortical thickening) when "diffuse hyperostosis" is asked.

## Non-accidental injury (NAI) — frequently its own question
- **Highly specific fractures:** **classic metaphyseal lesions (CML) — "corner" / "bucket-handle"**, **posterior rib fractures**, scapular, sternal, and spinous-process fractures.
- **Moderate specificity:** multiple fractures (esp. bilateral), fractures of **different ages**, complex/diastatic skull fractures, vertebral.
- **Skeletal survey protocol** (dedicated views, not a babygram); repeat survey at ~2 weeks increases yield.
- **Dating** by periosteal reaction (5-14 d), callus, remodelling.
- **Differentials to exclude:** OI, rickets/metabolic, physiological periostitis of infancy, Menkes, birth trauma.

## Pearls / buzzwords
- "Interpedicular distance decreasing caudally" → achondroplasia.
- "Wormian bones" → OI, cleidocranial dysplasia, pyknodysostosis, hypothyroidism, etc. (mnemonic PORKCHOPS).
- "Corner/bucket-handle metaphyseal fracture" → NAI until proven otherwise.

## What to draw
Achondroplasia lumbar spine (narrowing interpedicular distance); metaphyseal corner/bucket-handle lesion.

## Practice questions

**Q1.** Discuss the role of **ultrasound** in the evaluation of the musculoskeletal system. `[10]`

**Q2.** Describe the MRI features that help characterise a **soft-tissue mass** and the imaging features suggesting malignancy. Outline principles of biopsy. `[4+4+2]`

**Q3.** Describe the imaging features of **peripheral nerve-sheath tumours** and how to differentiate schwannoma from neurofibroma. `[6+4]`

**Q4.** Short notes: (a) PVNS / GCT of tendon sheath `[5]`  (b) Lipomatous tumours — imaging `[5]`

**Q5.** Discuss the role of imaging in a suspected **soft-tissue sarcoma** including staging and follow-up. `[10]`

## Model-answer pointers

- **Q1:** Tendons, muscle injury, effusion/synovitis (power Doppler), foreign body, guidance, paediatric hip; advantages/limits.
- **Q2:** Signal characteristics, fat-suppression behaviour, enhancement; malignancy = >5 cm, deep, heterogeneous, necrosis; biopsy after MRI, tract through resection field.
- **Q3:** Fusiform along nerve, split-fat/target/fascicular/entering-exiting; schwannoma eccentric/encapsulated vs neurofibroma central; plexiform → NF1; MPNST features.
- **Q4:** (a) haemosiderin → blooming on GRE, low T1/T2. (b) follows fat, suppresses; atypical features → liposarcoma.
- **Q5:** MRI local staging (compartment, NV bundle), chest CT (lung mets), grading, biopsy; follow-up for local recurrence/mets; PET role.

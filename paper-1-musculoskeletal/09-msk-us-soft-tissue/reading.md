# MSK Ultrasound & Soft-Tissue / Nerve-Sheath Lesions

## Musculoskeletal ultrasound — uses
- **Tendons:** tendinosis (hypoechoic thickening, neovascularity on power Doppler), partial/full tears, dynamic assessment (impingement, subluxation).
- **Muscle injury:** grading (I strain, II partial tear, III complete), haematoma.
- **Joints:** effusion, synovitis with **power Doppler** (activity in inflammatory arthritis), guidance for aspiration/injection.
- **Other:** foreign body detection (echogenic ± posterior shadow), ganglion vs solid, nerve assessment, paediatric hip (DDH — Graf, and effusion).
- Advantages: no radiation, dynamic, high resolution for superficial structures, real-time guidance. Limits: operator-dependent, bone/deep penetration poor.

## Soft-tissue masses — approach
- MRI is the primary modality for characterisation/staging; **few lesions have a specific imaging diagnosis** (lipoma, haemangioma, certain cysts). **Imaging cannot reliably exclude sarcoma.**
- **Features suggesting malignancy:** size **>5 cm**, **deep to deep fascia**, heterogeneity, necrosis, rapid growth, ill-defined margins, peritumoral oedema.
- **Plan biopsy after MRI**, with tract through the future surgical resection field (discuss with operating surgeon) — never compromise limb salvage.

**Specific entities (one line each)**
- **Lipoma:** follows fat on all sequences, suppresses with fat-sat; atypical/liposarcoma if thick septa, nodularity, non-fatty components.
- **Haemangioma:** serpentine vessels, **phleboliths**, fat overgrowth.
- **GCT of tendon sheath / PVNS:** **blooming on GRE (haemosiderin)**, low T1/T2.
- **Myxoma:** very high T2, low T1, mild enhancement.
- **Synovial sarcoma:** juxta-articular, may calcify, **"triple sign"** and fluid-fluid levels; despite name, not from synovium.

## Nerve-sheath tumours (schwannoma vs neurofibroma)
- Both: **fusiform mass in line with a nerve**.
- **MRI signs:** **split-fat sign** (rim of fat at poles), **target sign** (central low + peripheral high T2), **fascicular sign**, **entering/exiting nerve**.
- **Schwannoma:** eccentric to nerve, encapsulated → resectable preserving the nerve.
- **Neurofibroma:** central (nerve runs through); **plexiform neurofibroma** is pathognomonic of **NF1**; malignant peripheral nerve sheath tumour (MPNST) if rapid growth, size, heterogeneity, central necrosis, in an NF1 patient.

## Pearls / buzzwords
- "Target sign / split-fat / entering-exiting nerve" → peripheral nerve-sheath tumour.
- "Blooming on GRE, low T1/T2 juxta-articular mass" → PVNS / GCT tendon sheath.
- Power Doppler synovitis = disease activity in inflammatory arthritis.

# Skeletal Changes in Leukaemia & Haemolytic Anaemia

> Unifying theme for haemolytic anaemias = **marrow hyperplasia / expansion**. Draw the skull.

## Leukaemia (children)
- **Leukaemic lines:** transverse **metaphyseal lucent bands** (also non-specific in any severe childhood illness).
- Diffuse **osteopenia**, **permeative/moth-eaten** destruction, **periostitis**, lytic lesions, vertebral collapse, metaphyseal lucencies; rarely sclerosis.
- DDx of permeative paediatric lesion: leukaemia, Ewing, metastatic neuroblastoma, Langerhans cell histiocytosis, osteomyelitis.

## Thalassaemia (marrow expansion is dramatic)
- Skull: **"hair-on-end" appearance**, widened **diploic space** with thinned outer table.
- Facial bones: maxillary marrow overgrowth → **"rodent facies"**, obliteration of paranasal sinuses (except ethmoids), dental malocclusion.
- Long bones / hands: widened medullary cavities, thinned cortices, coarse trabeculae, **Erlenmeyer-flask** modelling, premature physeal fusion.
- **Extramedullary haematopoiesis:** paravertebral soft-tissue masses, hepatosplenomegaly.
- **Secondary haemochromatosis** (transfusions): organ iron (low T2* liver/heart on MRI), arthropathy, chondrocalcinosis.

## Sickle cell disease
- **Vertebrae: "H-shaped" / "fish-mouth" (Lincoln-log)** central endplate depressions (microvascular infarction of growth).
- **Bone infarcts** (medullary "smoke-ring"/serpentine calcification) and **avascular necrosis** of femoral & humeral heads.
- **Dactylitis (hand-foot syndrome)** in infants.
- **Osteomyelitis** — increased risk; **Salmonella** is classic (also *S. aureus*); differentiating acute infarct from osteomyelitis is a clinical/imaging challenge (MRI, sequential bone marrow + bone scan).
- Marrow hyperplasia (less marked than thalassaemia), **autosplenectomy** (small calcified spleen), cardiomegaly.

## MRI marrow
- Chronic anaemia → **red-marrow reconversion** → diffusely low T1 marrow (darker than/near muscle); helps explain the imaging.

## Pearls / buzzwords
- "Hair-on-end skull + rodent facies" → thalassaemia. "H-shaped vertebrae + AVN + dactylitis" → sickle cell.
- "Salmonella osteomyelitis" → sickle cell association.

## What to draw
Lateral skull "hair-on-end"; H-shaped (Lincoln-log) vertebra.

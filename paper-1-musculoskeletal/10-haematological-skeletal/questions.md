## Practice questions

**Q1.** Describe the skeletal manifestations of **thalassaemia**. `[10]`

**Q2.** Discuss the radiological findings in **sickle cell disease**, including how you would differentiate **bone infarct from osteomyelitis**. `[6+4]`

**Q3.** Describe the skeletal changes seen in **leukaemia** in children and the differential diagnosis of a permeative lesion. `[6+4]`

**Q4.** Short notes: (a) Extramedullary haematopoiesis — imaging `[5]`  (b) Hair-on-end skull — causes `[5]`

**Q5.** Discuss the role of MRI in the assessment of **bone marrow** in haematological disease (incl. iron-overload). `[10]`

## Model-answer pointers

- **Q1:** Hair-on-end skull, rodent facies, widened medullary cavities/coarse trabeculae, Erlenmeyer flask, extramedullary haematopoiesis, secondary haemochromatosis.
- **Q2:** H-shaped vertebrae, infarcts, AVN, dactylitis, autosplenectomy; infarct vs OM (sequential marrow oedema + soft-tissue/sinus, dual-isotope bone-marrow scan, MRI).
- **Q3:** Leukaemic lines, osteopenia, permeative destruction, periostitis; DDx Ewing, neuroblastoma mets, LCH, osteomyelitis.
- **Q4:** (a) paravertebral lobulated soft-tissue masses, fat-containing, in thalassaemia; no bone destruction. (b) thalassaemia, sickle cell, other chronic anaemias, cyanotic heart disease.
- **Q5:** Red/yellow marrow on T1, reconversion (low T1), infiltration patterns; iron overload → low T2*/signal (liver/heart), R2* quantification.

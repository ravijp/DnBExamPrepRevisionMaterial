## Practice questions

**Q1.** Describe the **MRI protocol** for the knee. Enumerate the primary and secondary signs of an **ACL tear**. `[3+(4+3)]`

**Q2.** Classify **meniscal tears** and describe the MRI signs of a **bucket-handle tear**. What is the role of MR arthrography? `[4+4+2]`

**Q3.** Describe the **normal age-related distribution and conversion of bone marrow** and how to distinguish marrow infiltration from normal marrow on MRI. `[5+5]`

**Q4.** Discuss the role of MRI in **avascular necrosis** including staging. `[10]`

**Q5.** Short notes: (a) Rotator cuff tear — MRI `[5]`  (b) Bone bruise patterns `[5]`

## Model-answer pointers

- **Q1:** PD/T2 FS 3 planes; primary (discontinuity/signal/slope) + secondary (anterior translation, deep notch, kissing bruise, PCL buckling, Segond).
- **Q2:** Horizontal/vertical(radial,longitudinal)/complex/bucket-handle; double-PCL, flipped-meniscus, absent-bow-tie, fragment-in-notch; MR arthro for post-op re-tear.
- **Q3:** Peripheral→axial conversion, ends last; T1 red marrow > muscle normally; darker than muscle = infiltration; STIR for oedema.
- **Q4:** Double-line sign, crescent sign, serpentine, Ficat/ARCO staging; causes; MRI most sensitive.
- **Q5:** (a) full vs partial thickness, retraction, fatty atrophy (Goutallier). (b) contusion pattern indicates mechanism (pivot-shift, dashboard, clip injury).

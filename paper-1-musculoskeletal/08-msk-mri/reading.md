# MSK MRI — Internal Derangement, Marrow, Joints

## Knee (the most-asked MRI protocol + findings question)
**Protocol:** PD/T2 fat-sat in 3 planes ± T1; sagittal & coronal for menisci/cruciates, axial for patellofemoral & retinacula.

**ACL tear**
- **Primary signs:** fibre discontinuity, increased intrasubstance T2 signal, abnormal flat/horizontal slope of ACL, non-visualisation.
- **Secondary signs (enumerate — examiners love these):** **anterior tibial translation**, **deep lateral femoral notch sign**, **bone bruise at lateral femoral condyle + posterolateral tibial plateau** (pivot-shift), **PCL buckling**, **Segond fracture** (lateral capsular avulsion), uncovered posterior horn lateral meniscus.

**Meniscal tears**
- Tear = signal reaching the articular surface. Types: horizontal, vertical (longitudinal/radial), complex, **bucket-handle** → **double-PCL sign, flipped-meniscus sign, absent-bow-tie sign, fragment-in-notch**.
- **MR arthrography:** for **post-operative meniscus / re-tear** (contrast entering a previously repaired tear) and for labral/cartilage assessment.

## Shoulder
- Rotator cuff: tendinosis vs partial- vs full-thickness tear (fluid-signal gap, retraction, muscle atrophy/fatty infiltration — Goutallier grade).
- Labrum: **Bankart** (antero-inferior), **SLAP** (superior), Hill-Sachs impaction — best on **MR arthrography**.

## Bone marrow
- **Normal red→yellow conversion** with age: proceeds from peripheral→axial and epiphyses/apophyses convert first; long-bone ends/proximal metaphyses retain red marrow longest.
- **Reconversion** (yellow→red) under marrow stress: chronic anaemia, marrow-stimulating factors, smokers, obesity.
- **Pathology on T1:** normal red marrow stays **brighter than skeletal muscle / disc**; marrow **darker than muscle on T1 = infiltration/replacement** (tumour, myeloma).
- **Marrow oedema pattern** (low T1, high STIR): trauma/bruise, infection, transient osteoporosis of hip, AVN, tumour, stress reaction.

## Avascular necrosis (AVN)
- MRI most sensitive. **Double-line sign** (T2 — inner high granulation + outer low sclerosis), serpentine subchondral low signal, **crescent sign** (subchondral collapse). Staging: **Ficat / ARCO**. Causes: steroids, alcohol, sickle cell, trauma, SLE, Gaucher, caisson disease.

## Cartilage imaging
- Sequences: fat-sat PD/T2, 3D GRE/DESS, T2 mapping/dGEMRIC (advanced). Grade chondral loss (Outerbridge/ICRS).

## Pearls / buzzwords
- "Double-PCL sign" → bucket-handle medial meniscal tear. "Deep lateral notch + kissing bone bruise" → ACL tear.
- "Double-line sign" → AVN. T1 marrow darker than muscle → think infiltration.

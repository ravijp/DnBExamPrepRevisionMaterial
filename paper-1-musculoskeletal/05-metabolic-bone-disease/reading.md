# Metabolic & Endocrine Bone Disease + Bone Density

## Rickets (children) & osteomalacia (adults) — same biochemistry, different skeleton
- **Rickets — changes at the growth plate (draw the wrist):** **cupping, fraying, splaying** of the metaphysis, **widened lucent (un-mineralised) physis**, frayed/irregular zone of provisional calcification.
- General: bowing of long bones, **rachitic rosary** (costochondral junctions), bossing, Harrison sulcus, greenstick fractures.
- **Osteomalacia:** osteopenia + **Looser zones (pseudofractures)** — lucent bands perpendicular to cortex at femoral neck, pubic rami, medial scapula, ribs.

## Hyperparathyroidism (enumerate the classic signs)
- **Subperiosteal bone resorption** — radial aspect of the middle phalanges (most specific); also tufts (acro-osteolysis), lamina dura, SI joints.
- **Brown tumours** (osteoclastomas — lytic, may be expansile).
- **"Salt-and-pepper" skull**, **"rugger-jersey" spine** (sclerotic endplate bands, esp. secondary/renal), chondrocalcinosis, vascular calcification.
- Primary (adenoma) vs **secondary** (CKD) vs tertiary.

## Renal osteodystrophy
- Combination: **osteomalacia + secondary HPT (resorption, brown tumours) + osteosclerosis (rugger-jersey) + soft-tissue/vascular & periarticular calcification**.

## Bone densitometry
- **DEXA** (dual-energy X-ray absorptiometry) at lumbar spine & hip.
- **WHO criteria (postmenopausal/>50 yr men):** **T-score >= -1 normal; -1 to -2.5 osteopenia; <= -2.5 osteoporosis.**
- **Z-score** (compares to age-matched) for premenopausal women, men <50, children; <= -2.0 = "below expected for age".
- Limitations: degenerative sclerosis/aortic calcification falsely raise spine BMD; QCT is an alternative (true volumetric, not affected by osteophytes).

## Other quick entities (one line each)
- **Scurvy** (vitamin C): **Wimberger ring** (dense epiphyseal rim), **Frankel line** (dense metaphyseal), **Pelkan spur**, **Trummerfeld/scurvy zone**, subperiosteal haemorrhage.
- **Paget disease:** 3 phases — lytic (osteoporosis circumscripta in skull) → mixed → sclerotic; **cortical thickening, trabecular coarsening, bone expansion**; "picture-frame" vertebra, "cotton-wool" skull; risk of sarcomatous transformation.
- **Acromegaly:** heel-pad thickening, enlarged sella, spade-like tufts, increased joint spaces.

## Pearls / buzzwords
- "Subperiosteal resorption, radial side of middle phalanx" → hyperparathyroidism (pathognomonic).
- "Looser zones" → osteomalacia. "Rugger-jersey spine" → renal osteodystrophy.
